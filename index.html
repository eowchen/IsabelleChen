The Competitor

Isabelle Chen has been wrestling since she was young — drawn to the discipline, the strategy, and the community that combat sport builds. Now 17, she trains with the Bhullar Wrestling Club in Richmond and competes at the national level in both freestyle wrestling and Brazilian jiu-jitsu.

In May 2026, she claimed gold at the Canadian Wrestling World Team Trials, earning her place on Canada's national team for the 2026 World Championship in Azerbaijan. It's a result built on years of early mornings, hard practices, and a refusal to take the easier path.

"Wrestling has given me everything — discipline, community, confidence. Getting to represent Canada on a world stage is something I've worked toward for years."

The Leader

Isabelle doesn't just compete — she builds. She founded her high school's first-ever wrestling team from scratch, recruiting athletes, organizing practices, and creating a space that hadn't existed before. She also serves as a junior coach, mentoring younger wrestlers through the same challenges she's navigated herself.

Her leadership extends off the mat too. As a member of Canada's Generation 2026 Youth Leadership Council, she uses her platform to advocate for equity, representation, and access for girls in sport.

Richmond Teenager Wins Gold at Canadian Wrestling World Team Trials, Earns Spot on National Team

Isabelle Chen, 17, will represent Canada at the 2026 World Championship in Azerbaijan this July

Richmond, BC — Isabelle Chen, a 17-year-old wrestler and student-athlete from Richmond, British Columbia, claimed gold at the 2026 Canadian Wrestling World Team Trials, securing her place on Canada's national team for the 2026 World Championship in Azerbaijan, scheduled for late July 2026.

Chen trains with the Bhullar Wrestling Club in Richmond and has been competing in wrestling and Brazilian jiu-jitsu since a young age. A two-time MVP and national medalist, she is also the founder of her high school's wrestling team and serves as a junior coach — building opportunities for the next generation of girls in combat sports.

"Wrestling has given me everything — discipline, community, confidence. Getting to represent Canada on a world stage is something I've worked toward for years, and I'm deeply grateful to everyone who has supported me along the way." — Isabelle Chen

In addition to her athletic achievements, Chen is a member of Canada's Generation 2026 Youth Leadership Council, where she advocates for equity and representation in sport. Her journey reflects a commitment to both competitive excellence and community leadership — proving that strength, compassion, and leadership go hand in hand.

To help fund the costs of travel, competition fees, and preparation for the World Championship, Chen's supporters have launched a community fundraising campaign with a goal of $10,000. Donations can be made through her GoFundMe page, and corporate sponsorship opportunities are also available.
